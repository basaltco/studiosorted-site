An MMA gym is administratively the hardest of the three club types on this site, for a reason that has nothing to do with fighting. You are running several distinct disciplines — BJJ, Muay Thai or striking, wrestling, conditioning, open mat — on one timetable, for members who have bought different levels of access to it. Some are on everything. Some do Tuesday and Thursday striking only. A handful are on a fight team that trains on top of all of it.

Then there is the rank problem. Your BJJ programme has belts and stripes. Your striking programme mostly does not. Tools built around a single belt ladder handle half your gym elegantly and the other half awkwardly.

Quick Verdict

Martialytics remains our starting recommendation for UK gyms on price and fit — GBP-native from £69/month for 50 students, purpose-built for martial arts, with grading readiness handled properly for the BJJ side. Test the membership tiers against your actual access levels during the free trial, because that is where multi-discipline gyms hit friction rather than on rank.

Gymdesk is the better answer if your membership structure is genuinely complicated or you have a lot of families, since family accounts, POS and skills tracking are all in the base price. Several specialist MMA-focused platforms also market multi-discipline scheduling and fight-team features — we have not verified those to the same standard as the tools below, so treat them as worth a demo rather than a recommendation.

What an MMA Gym Needs That a Dojo Tool Misses

One timetable, several disciplines. BJJ, striking, wrestling and conditioning need to be distinct class types with their own registers, not one undifferentiated schedule.

Membership tiers that match access. Discipline-specific membership alongside unlimited access is the norm in MMA and rare in a traditional dojo. Your software has to stop a striking-only member booking into the Wednesday gi class without you policing it manually.

Fight team as a layer, not a category. Competitive fighters are ordinary members with extra structure over the top — camp scheduling, weight tracking, event admin. Tools that force a binary between “fighter” and “member” get this wrong.

Rank that applies to some programmes only. Belt and stripe tracking for the BJJ side; typically nothing equivalent for striking. A tool that insists on a rank for every member creates empty fields you learn to ignore.

Check this before anything else if you are a UK club: how the tool takes money. Most of these platforms are American and route payments through card processors. A great many UK clubs collect monthly fees by Direct Debit, commonly through GoCardless, because it is cheaper than card fees and fails less often on recurring payments. None of the tools in this comparison has confirmed GoCardless support, and Martialytics documentation lists it explicitly as not yet compatible. Confirm the migration path with the vendor before you commit, not after.

Who This Is NOT For

This page is for people running an MMA or combat sports gym as a business. It is not for:

Pure BJJ academies — if grappling is all you run, the stripe-tracking question matters more than multi-discipline scheduling. See our BJJ page instead.

Traditional dojos — a single-discipline kyu ladder has different needs again.

Individual coaches — if you rent mat space and coach a handful of fighters, none of this repays its cost.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can these tools stop a striking-only member booking a BJJ class?

This is the question to test in a trial rather than take on trust. All four tools support distinct class types and membership plans, but how strictly a plan restricts booking varies. Set up your real membership tiers during the free trial and try to book something a member should not have access to — it takes ten minutes and it is the most common source of friction in a multi-discipline gym.

How do I handle belts for BJJ when striking has no ranks?

Run rank as something that applies to the BJJ programme rather than to the member globally, where the tool allows it. In practice most gyms record the belt on the student profile and simply leave it unused for striking-only members. It is untidy but harmless — the more useful thing is making sure grading readiness draws on BJJ attendance only, not total attendance across all disciplines.

Is there software for managing a fight team specifically?

Several platforms market fight-camp and fighter-tracking features. We have not verified them to the standard used for the main comparison, so we are not recommending a specific one. What we would say is that fight-team admin is usually a small number of people with high-touch needs — be realistic about whether that justifies choosing your whole gym’s platform around it.

What does MMA gym software cost in the UK?

Broadly £60–£200 per month for a gym of 50 to 150 members, plus payment processing of roughly 1–2% on top of the card processor’s own fee. USD-priced tools move with the exchange rate, which is worth factoring in if you are comparing a dollar price against Martialytics’ pound one.
